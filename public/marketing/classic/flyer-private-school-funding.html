Government & State Funding Sources

Private schools may offset platform licenses, onboarding, professional development, and kiosk hardware through federal equitable services and state reimbursement programs. Direct your district or state coordinator to the applicable funding codes below.

Title IV-A

Applies platform license costs toward Student Support and Academic Enrichment (SSAE): safe schools, healthy students, and PBIS incentive platforms.

Title II-A

Applies onboarding and teacher professional development ($950–$1,950, scales with enrollment) toward system integration workshops and school culture training.

Title I

Eligible under school improvement and parent engagement for building-wide behavior systems, attendance visibility, and family-facing progress.

Equitable Services

Private and nonpublic schools receive proportional federal Title IV-A services through the local district nonpublic coordinator—request an equitable services purchase order.

NYS MSR

Mandated Services Reimbursement (PAR): automate pupil attendance reporting with class sign-in logs and kiosk check-ins eligible for state reimbursement.

NYS NPSE

Nonpublic Safety Equipment grants may cover arrival accountability kiosks, entry scanning hardware, and campus safety visibility tied to attendance workflows.

Micro-Purchase

Sole-source and micro-purchase paths may apply for annual contracts under local procurement thresholds (often up to $25,000)—confirm with your business office.
